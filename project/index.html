Regional Climate Change

Climate Stripes. Series of coloured stripes chronologically ordered to visually shows long-term anomaly from 1750 to 2020. The gray stripes represents the missing data.

The linechart shows the trend of the temperatures for the years available for the selected country. Temperatures are in Celsius and reported as anomalies relative to the Jan 1951-Dec 1980 average. Uncertainties represent the 95% confidence interval for statistical noise and spatial undersampling effects. Such uncertainties are expected to account for the effects of random noise as well as random biases affecting station trends and random shifts in station baselines. If the rage of year, selected from the left sidebar is higher than 1 is showed the comparision between the annual and the data averaged on the selected year.

Mean Rate of Change ( °C / Year )

Table with mean rate of change of the temperature every 50 years. The light blue cells represent the first available temperature. In the other cells, each values represent the annual temperature increasement of the previous 50 years.

Anomalies Records

The linecharts represent the top 5 hottest years and the top 5 coldest year along all available years. The gray lines are all the other plotted one every 5 years.

Seasonal Changes

The linechart represents the seasonal changes. The black line represents the average temperature from 1951 to 1980, and the gray area represents the 95% of confidence. The dashed lines are the max-min range temperatures from the first to the last year available. The lines red, green, blue represent the monthly temperatures of the last three available years.
